My Journey to CS

During my undergrad, I remember being drawn to computer science, but being intimidated by the math prerequisites. I decided instead to pursue a Bachelor's in Linguistics, another passion of mine. After graduating in 2014, I found a writing/editing role at an e-learning startup and since then have been working in similar roles.

I figured that ship had sailed until around 2019 when I learned about the Northeastern MSCS Align Program, which is designed for people who didn't major in CS in their undergrad. Almost a whole decade had passed since I'd last considered pursuing a STEM field, and I was completely different from the young girl in 2010 who was too scared to take calculus. Years of experience had given me the wisdom to know that fundamentally, I was no different than anyone else who studies computer science. I just needed the guts to work at it.

After mulling it over for a couple more years, I finally decided to go for it and enrolled in the program in 2021.
