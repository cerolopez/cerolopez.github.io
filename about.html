I am currently seeking full-time employment in web content management or strategy, frontend web development, UI engineering, product management, or similar role.

My Journey to CS

During my undergrad, I remember being drawn to computer science, but being intimidated by the math. It didn't come naturally to me and I didn't have the confidence to keep at it. I decided instead to pursue a Bachelor's in Linguistics, another passion of mine. After graduating in 2014, I found a job as a Content Editor and since then have been working in similar roles.

In 2019, I was excited to learn that you could earn a Master's in Computer Science without a background in CS through the Northeastern Align Program. Being surrounded by such brilliant research scientists and engineers at Meta (where I worked back then), I was inspired to go for it. In August 2021, I decided to apply at the last minute, got accepted about a week later, and enrolled the next month.

For about a year, I continued to work full-time at Meta while in grad school full-time. However, my time at Meta came to an unexpected end when my team was impacted by the first round of layoffs at the end of 2021. Although I was devastated, I took this opportunity to focus my full attention on finishing school. I graduated in May 2024 and since then have been working part-time while I figure out what to do next.

I'm eager to apply my uniquely diverse range of skills to a new full-time role at a mission-driven company. I am passionate about creating beautiful, frictionless user experiences guided by empathy and strategic visual design.
